# Business-Analysis
## 📄 Ethical Impact Assessment

This ethical impact analysis evaluates Microsoft’s Dragon AI tool, a clinical documentation system designed to streamline physician workflows through speech-to-text transcription, automated charting, and AI-assisted summarization. While the system offers clear operational benefits—such as reducing the time burden associated with electronic health record documentation—it also introduces critical ethical considerations within healthcare environments.

The analysis focuses on three primary areas of concern: lack of transparency in training data, risks to patient confidentiality, and potential AI dependency among healthcare professionals. The “black box” nature of training data raises concerns about algorithmic bias, particularly for underrepresented populations. Additionally, research shows that anonymized health data may still be re-identified, posing privacy risks. Overreliance on AI systems may also reduce critical thinking and professional oversight in clinical decision-making.

Using ethical frameworks such as virtue ethics, deontological ethics, and teleological ethics, this analysis highlights the responsibility of developers and healthcare providers to ensure secure, transparent, and responsible AI use. It emphasizes the need for stronger data transparency, enhanced privacy protections, and clear organizational policies.
